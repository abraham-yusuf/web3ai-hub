# Gas Fees: Why You Pay and How to Save

Every time you send crypto, swap tokens, or buy an NFT, there's a fee you must pay — called a **gas fee**. This fee can be small (a few cents) or very large (tens of dollars), depending on network conditions.

## What is a Gas Fee?

A **gas fee** is the cost you pay to execute a transaction on the blockchain. Think of it like **shipping cost** — to send a package, you pay the courier.

On the blockchain:
- **Transaction** = The package being sent
- **Miner/Validator** = The courier processing it
- **Gas fee** = The shipping cost

## Why Do Gas Fees Exist?

1. **Prevent spam** — Without fees, someone could flood the network with fake transactions
2. **Reward validators** — Miners/validators use computing power to process transactions
3. **Allocate resources** — Blockchain has limited capacity; fees help prioritize who goes first

## How Gas Fees Work

### Unit: Gwei
Gas fees are measured in **Gwei** (billionths of ETH):
- 1 ETH = 1,000,000,000 Gwei
- 1 Gwei = 0.000000001 ETH

### Components (EIP-1559)
1. **Base Fee** — Minimum cost set by the network (burned)
2. **Priority Fee (Tip)** — Extra tip to validators for faster processing
3. **Gas Limit** — Maximum gas units you're willing to pay

### Fee Comparison Across Networks

| Network | Avg Fee | Speed |
|---------|---------|-------|
| Ethereum | $1-50 | 12-15 sec |
| Arbitrum | $0.10-1 | 1-2 sec |
| Polygon | $0.01-0.10 | 2 sec |
| BNB Chain | $0.10-0.50 | 3 sec |
| Solana | $0.001-0.01 | 0.4 sec |

## Strategies to Save on Gas Fees

### 1. Time Your Transactions
Fees are lower during weekends, late night (US/Europe time), and when the market is quiet.

### 2. Use Layer 2
Layer 2s are "express lanes" on top of Ethereum — **Arbitrum**, **Optimism**, **Base**, **zkSync** offer fees 10-100x cheaper.

### 3. Batch Transactions
Combine multiple operations into one when possible.

### 4. Use Alternative Blockchains
For non-Ethereum-critical transactions: Polygon for NFTs/gaming, Solana for fast trading, BNB Chain for cheap DeFi.

### 5. Check Gas Before Transacting
Use tools like etherscan.io/gastracker to monitor real-time gas prices.

## Tips for Beginners

1. **Start on Layer 2** — Arbitrum or Polygon for learning
2. **Check gas before confirming** — Don't blindly click confirm
3. **Don't make small transactions on Ethereum mainnet** — Fee can exceed the transaction amount
4. **Keep native tokens** — Always have some ETH/MATIC for fees
5. **Be patient** — Wait for gas to drop if not urgent

## Key Takeaways

- Gas fees are transaction costs on blockchain
- Paid to miners/validators as compensation
- Fees increase with network congestion and transaction complexity
- Save with: good timing, Layer 2, batching, alternative chains
- Beginners should start on Layer 2 to avoid expensive fees
