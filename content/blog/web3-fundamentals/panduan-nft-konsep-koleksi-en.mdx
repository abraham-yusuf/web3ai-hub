# NFT Guide: From Concept to Collecting

You've probably heard about NFTs selling for millions, or pixel art priced like houses. But what actually are NFTs? Is this just hype, or genuinely useful technology?

This article explains NFTs from basic concepts to starting your collection — no FOMO, no excessive jargon.

## What is an NFT?

**NFT** stands for **Non-Fungible Token** — a unique digital asset that proves your ownership of something on the blockchain.

Let's break it down:

- **Non-Fungible** = Cannot be exchanged 1:1 with another. A $100 bill can be swapped for two $50s — that's **fungible**. But the Mona Lisa can't be swapped for another painting — that's **non-fungible**.
- **Token** = A digital record on the blockchain that proves something.

So an NFT is a **digital proof of ownership** that's unique and tamper-proof.

## What Can Be an NFT?

NFTs aren't just for JPEG images. They can represent:

- **Digital art** — Illustrations, photography, generative art
- **Music** — Songs, albums, concert tickets
- **Gaming** — In-game items, characters, skins
- **Domains** — Decentralized domain names (like .eth)
- **Certificates** — Diplomas, licenses, credentials
- **Tickets** — Events, conferences, memberships

## How NFTs Work

### Smart Contract ERC-721

NFTs on Ethereum use the **ERC-721** standard. The smart contract records who owns each token, enables ownership transfers, and stores metadata (link to image, description, etc.).

### Metadata and Storage

NFT images are typically **not stored directly on blockchain** (too large). Instead:
- Images stored on IPFS (decentralized) or regular servers
- Blockchain stores only the **hash** and **link** to the image

### Minting

**Minting** is the process of creating a new NFT. You upload your work, fill in metadata, and the smart contract creates a new token on the blockchain with you as the first owner.

## How to Buy Your First NFT

1. **Set up a wallet** — Install MetaMask or another Web3 wallet
2. **Choose a marketplace** — OpenSea is a good starting point
3. **Research** — Check floor price, volume, utility, community
4. **Buy** — Connect wallet, select NFT, click "Buy" or "Place Bid"

## NFT Risks

- **Extreme volatility** — Prices can swing 10x in a week
- **Low liquidity** — NFTs can be very hard to sell
- **Scams and phishing** — Fake collections, phishing links, wash trading
- **Rug pulls** — Projects that disappear after collecting funds

## The Future of NFTs

NFTs aren't just about expensive art. The technology can be used for:
- **Ticketing** — Tamper-proof concert tickets
- **Identity** — Portable digital identity
- **Real estate** — Fractional property ownership
- **Supply chain** — Product authenticity certificates

## Key Takeaways

- NFTs are unique digital proofs of ownership on blockchain
- Use cases: art, music, gaming, domains, certificates, tickets
- How to buy: wallet → marketplace → research → buy
- Risks: volatility, low liquidity, scams, rug pulls
- NFT technology has potential far beyond digital art
