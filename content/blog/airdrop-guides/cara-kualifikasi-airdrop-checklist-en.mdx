## Complete Airdrop Qualification Checklist

Crypto airdrops can be a significant source of income for Web3 users. However, many people fail to qualify because they don't follow the right steps. This article provides a complete checklist so you're ready to receive airdrops from any project.

---

### 1. Wallet Setup

The first and most fundamental step is setting up the right wallet.

- **Use a self-custody wallet** like MetaMask, Rabby, or Keplr
- **Store your seed phrase offline** — never screenshot or save it to the cloud
- **Set up multi-chain wallets**: Ethereum, Arbitrum, Optimism, Base, Solana, Cosmos, and more
- **Create separate wallets** for airdrop activities to distribute risk
- **Keep wallets active for at least 3-6 months** before common snapshot dates

**Pro tip:** Some projects give more weight to wallets that have been active for a long time compared to new wallets.

---

### 2. Testnet Participation

Testnets are one of the most effective ways to qualify for airdrops at minimal cost.

- **Check active testnets** on platforms like [testnet.web3ai-hub.com](https://testnet.web3ai-hub.com)
- **Mint testnet tokens** from official faucets (don't use suspicious third-party faucets)
- **Perform testnet transactions** regularly — at least 5-10 transactions per week
- **Interact with all available features** on the testnet (swap, bridge, lending, staking)
- **Submit bug reports** if you find issues — some projects offer special rewards
- **Document wallet addresses** used on testnets

**Success stories:** EigenLayer, StarkNet, and zkSync gave massive airdrops to early testnet participants.

---

### 3. Mainnet Activity

After a project launches on mainnet, make sure you stay active.

- **Perform regular transactions** at least once a week
- **Diversify your activities** — don't just swap, try lending, borrowing, LP, and staking
- **Use dApps directly** rather than through aggregators to ensure interactions are recorded
- **Hold assets in protocols** for extended periods (holding duration is often a factor)
- **Avoid sybil-looking activity** — don't perform identical transactions from many wallets

---

### 4. Social Tasks

Many projects require social activity as part of qualification.

- **Follow official accounts** on Twitter/X, Discord, and Telegram
- **Retweet and engage** with project posts organically
- **Join Discord** and be active in relevant channels
- **Fill out registration forms** if available (often a mandatory requirement)
- **Verify roles** on Discord using bots like Guild.xyz
- **Participate in governance forums** if the project already has a DAO

**Warning:** Don't use bots or fake engagement services — projects are getting increasingly sophisticated at detecting spam accounts.

---

### 5. DeFi Interactions

Diverse DeFi interactions show that you're a real user.

- **Perform swaps** on the project's native DEX (not just Uniswap)
- **Provide liquidity** in relevant pools
- **Use lending/borrowing** if available
- **Stake tokens** in the protocol for extended periods
- **Try new features** when projects launch new products — early adopters often receive rewards
- **Use advanced features** like limit orders if available

---

### 6. Bridge Usage

Bridge activity is often an additional criterion in airdrop snapshots.

- **Bridge assets** from L1 to L2 (e.g., Ethereum to Arbitrum)
- **Use the project's official bridge** if available
- **Bridge regularly** with varying amounts
- **Try cross-chain bridges** between L2s (Arbitrum to Optimism, etc.)
- **Document all bridge transactions** for future claim reference

**Popular bridges often used as airdrop criteria:** LayerZero, Wormhole, Stargate, Across.

---

### 7. DAO and Governance

Governance participation shows long-term commitment to a project.

- **Vote on relevant proposals** on Snapshot or on-chain
- **Delegate voting power** to active delegates if you don't want to vote directly
- **Create proposals** if you have constructive ideas
- **Discuss in governance forums** like Discourse
- **Participate in multisigs** if invited as a signer

---

### 8. Documentation and Tracking

Good documentation helps you stay organized and not miss opportunities.

- **Create a spreadsheet** with all wallet addresses and activities performed
- **Record important dates** like form deadlines, snapshots, and claim periods
- **Use tracking tools** like DeBank or Zapper to monitor positions
- **Save transaction proofs** (TX hashes) for claims that require verification
- **Subscribe to project newsletters** for the latest updates
- **Bookmark official claim pages** to avoid phishing scams

---

### Key Takeaways

- Start with testnets — low cost, high reward potential
- Consistency matters more than volume — regular activity over months is more valuable than a burst
- Diversify interactions — don't just swap, use all available features
- Document everything — you never know when a snapshot will be taken
- Avoid suspicious sybil behavior — projects are getting smarter at detection
